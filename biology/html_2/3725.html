Karyopherin-Î²2 Inhibits and Reverses Aggregation and Liquid-liquid Phase Separation of the ALS/FTD-Associated Protein FUS

Authors: Emma Robinson, James Shorter, Lin Guo

Abstract

The study of RNA-binding proteins (RBP) offers insight into the mechanisms of pathologic protein aggregation in neurodegenerative diseases. We developed a protocol for purifying an RBP FUS and a nuclear import receptor (NIR) KapÎ²2 and testing the ability of KapÎ²2 to mitigate FUS aggregation and liquid-liquid phase separation.

Keywords: FUS, Liquid-liquid phase separation, Nuclear import receptor, KapÎ²2, Protein aggregation

Background

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) and frontotemporal dementia (FTD) are neurodegenerative diseases characterized by the mislocalization and aggregation of several RNA-binding proteins (RBP) (Mackenzie et al., 2010; Da Cruz and Cleveland, 2011; King et al., 2012). One of these proteins is FUS (Fused in Sarcoma), a nuclear protein mislocalizes in the cytoplasm of neurons in ALS/FTD patients, where it undergoes liquid-liquid phase transition followed by aberrant phase transition to form insoluble aggregates (Altmeyer et al., 2015; Burke et al., 2015; Lin et al., 2015; Molliex et al., 2015; Murakami et al., 2015; Patel et al., 2015) (Figure 1). Karyopherin-Î²2 (KapÎ²2), a nuclear import receptor (NIR), has been established as a chaperone and disaggregase of proteins with PY-nuclear localization sequences (NLS), such as FUS (Guo et al., 2018; Hofweber et al., 2018; Yoshizawa, et al., 2018). The following protocol was developed to test the ability of KapÎ²2 to inhibit and reverse FUS aggregation and phase separation ex vivo (Guo et al., 2018).

### Procedure

  1. FUS Purification
    1. Generate a FUS expression construct in a pDuet vector with a TEV-cleavable site, resulting in a GST-TEV-FUS construct. Express and purify GST-TEV-FUS protein from E. coli BL21-CodonPlus(DE3)-RIL cells (Agilent) as follow.
    2. Grow FUS-expressing cells in 6 L of Lennox Broth (LB) at 37 Â°C with shaking at 250 rpm until the OD600 is between 0.4-0.6. Induce cells with 1 ml of 1 M IPTG (final conc. 1 mM IPTG), then grow overnight at 15 Â°C with shaking at 250 rpm.
    3. Spin cells down at 4,000 rpm for 20 min at 4 Â°C. To lyse cells, resuspend pellet in FUS Wash Buffer (Recipe 1), add 20 Âµg of lysozyme per 1 ml resuspended cells, and incubate on ice for 30 min. Sonicate the cells for 4 min at amplitude 35 for 30 s intervals on/off. And then spin lysed cells down at 16,000 rpm for 20 min at 4 Â°C.
    4. To bind and separate GST-tagged proteins, 5 ml Glutathione Sepharose 4 Fast Flow beads (GE Healthcare) is added to lysate supernatant and the mixture was incubated for 90 min at 4 Â°C. Centrifuge beads at 1,792 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C and discard the supernatant. Wash beads three times with Wash Buffer, centrifuging at 1,124 x g for 2 min at 4 Â°C.
    5. Elute FUS by adding 5 ml FUS Elution Buffer (Recipe 2) and incubating for 20 min at room temperature. Centrifuge the beads in Elution Buffer at 1,124 x g for 2 min at 4 Â°C in Bio-spin chromatography columns (Bio-Rad) over glass tubes to collect the elution.
    6. Measure purified FUS via Bradford Assay. Flash freeze purified FUS in liquid nitrogen and store at -80 Â°C. Protein can be used for up to 3 years. Freeze-thaw cycle should be minimized.

  2. KapÎ²2 Purification
    1. Generate a KapÎ²2 expression construct in a pGEX-TEV vector with a TEV-cleavable site, resulting in a GST-TEV-KapÎ²2 construct. Express and purify this KapÎ²2 construct from E. coli BL21-CodonPlus(DE3)-RIL cells (Agilent) as follow.
    2. Grow KapÎ²2-expressing cells in 6 L of Lennox Broth (LB) at 37Â°C with shaking at 250 rpm until the OD600 is between 0.6-0.8. Induce cells with 1 ml of 1 M IPTG (final conc. 1 mM IPTG), then grow overnight at 25 Â°C with shaking at 250 rpm. Spin cells down at 4,000 rpm for 20 min at 4 Â°C.
    3. To lyse cells, resuspend pellet in Resuspension Buffer (Recipe 3), add 20 Âµg of lysozyme per 1 ml resuspended cells, and incubate on ice for 30 min. Sonicate cells for 1 min 30 s at amplitude 15, 30 s on/off, and then spin lysed cells down at 16,000 rpm for 60 min at 4 Â°C.
    4. Incubate lysate supernatant with 5 ml Glutathione Sepharose 4 Fast Flow beads (GE Healthcare) for 90 min at 4 Â°C. Centrifuge beads at 4,500 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C and discard the supernatant.
    5. Wash beads four times with 25 ml Resuspension Buffer each, by centrifuging at 1,124 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C. Add 10 ml ATP Buffer (Recipe 4) and let stand at room temperature for 10 min before centrifuging at 1,124 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C. Wash beads twice with 20 ml ATP Buffer each, by centrifuging at 1,124 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C. Wash beads three time with 10 ml Buffer A each time (Recipe 5), by centrifuging at 1,124 x g for 3 min at 4 Â°C.
    6. Elute KapÎ²2 by adding 20 ml KapÎ²2 Elution Buffer (Recipe 6) and incubating for 30 min at 4 Â°C. Centrifuge the beads in Elution Buffer at 1,124 x g for 2 min at 4 Â°C in Bio-spin chromatography columns (Bio-Rad) over glass tubes to collect the elution.
    7. Measure the concentration of the purified KapÎ²2, then add 1 Âµg TEV per 10 Âµg KapÎ²2. Aliquot the mixture into Low-Bind Tubes and incubate overnight at 30 Â°C to cleave the GST tag.
    8. Cleaved KapÎ²2 is further purified by HiTrap-Q anion exchange chromatography column (GE healthcare).

  3. Inhibition of FUS aggregation by KapÎ²2
    1. Spin down FUS at 16,100 x g for 10 min at 4 Â°C and transfer supernatant to a fresh tube. Measure the concentration of FUS and KapÎ²2 via Bradford Assay with calibration curve generated using BSA.
    2. Set up the aggregation assay in a 96-well plate, with a blank well, a standard FUS well, and experimental wells.
      1. In each experimental well, add 4 ÂµM FUS (Table 1), 4 ÂµM KapÎ²2 (Table 1), 5 Âµl TEV Buffer (Recipe 7), 1.5 Âµl 100 mM DTT, and 0.8 Âµg of TEV. Fill to 100 Âµl with FUS Buffer (Recipe 2). Pipet up and down gently to mix.
      2. In the blank well, add 5 Âµl TEV Buffer, 1.5 Âµl 100 mM DTT, and 0.8 Âµg TEV. Match the volume of KapÎ²2 added to the experimental wells with KapÎ²2 Buffer. Fill to 100 Âµl with FUS Buffer. Pipet up and down gently to mix.
      3. In the standard FUS well, add 4 ÂµM FUS, 5 Âµl TEV Buffer, 1.5 Âµl 100 mM DTT, and 0.8 Âµg TEV. Match the volume of KapÎ²2 added to the experimental wells with KapÎ²2 Buffer. Fill to 100 Âµl with FUS Buffer. Pipet up and down gently to mix.
    3. Set up the TECAN plate reader. Identify the plate type as Costar 96 Flat Black with no lid and no humidity cassette. Program for a kinetic loop of 90 cycles with fixed intervals of 00:01:00 with no shaking. Program absorbance readings at 395 nm at each cycle. Insert the plate into the plate reader and run the aggregation assay at room temperature.
    4. After 1.5 h, the data will be automatically stored in an excel spreadsheet (Figure 3).

  4. Disaggregation of FUS fibrils by KapÎ²2
    1. Spin down FUS at 16,100 x g for 10 min at 4 Â°C and transfer supernatant to a fresh tube. Measure the concentration of FUS and the concentration of KapÎ²2.
    2. Set up the aggregation assay in a 96-well plate, with a blank well, a standard FUS well, and experimental wells.
      1. In each experimental well, add 4 ÂµM FUS (Table 1), 5 Âµl TEV Buffer (Recipe 7), 1.5 Âµl 100 mM DTT, and 0.8 Âµg of TEV. Fill with FUS Buffer (Recipe 2) so that adding 4 ÂµM KapÎ²2 would give a total volume of 100 Âµl (Table 1). Pipet up and down gently to mix.

      3. In the standard FUS well, add 4 ÂµM FUS, 5 Âµl TEV Buffer, 1.5 Âµl 100 mM DTT, and 0.8 Âµg TEV. Fill with FUS Buffer so that adding 4 ÂµM KapÎ²2 would give a total volume of 100 Âµl. Pipet up and down gently to mix.
    3. Set up the TECAN plate reader. Identify the plate type as Costar 96 Flat Black with no lid and no humidity cassette. Program for a kinetic loop of 90 cycles with fixed intervals of 00:01:00.     4. At the end of the 90 cycles, add 4 ÂµM of KapÎ²2 to each experimental well. Add KapÎ²2 Buffer to the blank well and the standard FUS well, match the volume of KapÎ²2.   5. KapÎ²2 reverse FUS liquid-liquid phase separation and aberrant phase transition
    1. Spin down FUS at 16,100 x g for 10 min at 4 Â°C and transfer supernatant to a fresh tube. Measure the concentration.
    2. Dilute FUS to a concentration of 10 ÂµM (Table 1) with FUS Buffer (Recipe 2). Pipette 30 Âµl of 10 ÂµM FUS into a fresh tube. Leave the tube on the bench at room temperature for approximately 2 h.
    3. Set up the LEICA microscope to observe the liquid droplets using a 100x oil immersion objective.
    4. Pipette 10 Âµl of the 10 ÂµM FUS sample onto the cover slide above the drop of oil.
    5. Observe liquid-liquid droplet formation. Droplets move fluidly and merge upon contact.
    6. Add 1 Âµl of 100 Î¼M KapÎ²2 (Table 1) into the 10Î¼l FUS sample on the coverslip to disassemble FUS liquid droplets and reverse FUS liquid-liquid phase separation (Figure 5A). KapÎ²2 can disassemble preformed FUS liquid droplets within 30 s.
    7. Liquid droplets age into hydrogel after 6 hours of incubation at room temperature. Add 10 Âµl of 10 ÂµM FUS sample onto the coverslip to observe hydrogel. ### Data analysis

Analysis of FUS Aggregation

  1. Upon completion of any turbidity assay, data will automatically be imported from Tecan SparkControl into Microsoft Excel.
  2. Plot the absorbances of each well as a function of time. Subtract absorbances of the blank well from that of the standard well and the experiment wells to get signal.
  3. Normalize the signals to the maximum signal of the standard reaction to determine the relative extent of aggregation/disaggregation (Figures 3 and 4).

### Recipes

  1. FUS Wash Buffer
    1. Make 1x PBS prior to purifying FUS
      1. Add 1 L MilliQ H2O to a 1 L bottle
      2. Add 5 Phosphate-Buffered Saline tablets to the MilliQ H2O
      3. Stir until the tablets have fully dissolved. Filter and store at 4 Â°C
    2. Add fresh reagents to 300 ml 1x PBS the day of purification
      1. Add 6 protease inhibitor tablets to 1x PBS 
      2. Stir until the tablets have fully dissolved
      3. Filter and store at 4 Â°C
  2. FUS Elution Buffer
    1. Add 1 ml 1 M Tris (pH 8) buffer to a 50 ml beaker

    3. Add 112.9 mg reduced glutathione [final conc. 364 ÂµM] to remove GST-tagged protein from the Glutathione Sepharose 4 Fast Flow beads

  3. KapÎ²2 Resuspension Buffer

      2. Add 5.844 g of NaCl [final conc. 100 mM]
      3. Add 2 ml of 0.5 M EDTA [final conc. 1 mM]
      4. Add 200 ml glycerol [final conc. 20%]
      5. Fill to 1 L with MilliQ water
      6. Stir until reagents have fully dissolved and pH to pH 7.5. Filter and store at 4 Â°C
    2. Add fresh reagents to 300 ml of stock the day of purification
      1. Add 600 Âµl 1 M DTT [final conc. 2 mM]

      3. Add 6 protease inhibitor tablets

  4. KapÎ²2 ATP Buffer
    1. Make a 1 L stock solution prior to purifying KapÎ²2
