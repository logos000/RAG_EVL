Viral Chromosome Conformation Capture (V3C) Assays for Identifying Trans-interaction Sites between Lytic Viruses and the Cellular Genome

Authors: Kinjal Majumder, Maria Boftsi, David J Pintel

Abstract

The folding mechanisms of the mammalian genome package our genetic material into the nucleus, and in doing so, dictate its appropriate replication and expression. Chromosome conformation capture technology has enabled the dissection of the folding principles of the cellular genome. This has led to a better understanding of the role played by architectural proteins in forming and dissolving 3D-chromatin-structure. These assays are based on the principle of crosslinking distant cellular sites that are proximal to each other in 3D space using formaldehyde followed by digestion of formed hybrid DNA junctions. Invading viruses, such as the lytic parvovirus Minute Virus of Mice (MVM), establish distinct replication centers within the nuclear environment at cellular sites that preferentially undergo DNA damage, but do not integrate into the cellular DNA. We have adapted chromosome conformation capture technology to study the trans-interaction between MVM and the cellular genome, which we have dubbed V3C, which can be extended to a whole-genome analysis we term V3C-seq. This protocol describes the procedure for performing, as well as analyzing V3C-seq assays, and can be adapted for mapping the cellular interaction sites of any non-integrating DNA virus.

Keywords: Chromosome conformation capture, Parvovirus, DNA damage response, Topologically associating domains, Fragile sites, Host-pathogen genome interactions

Background

Chromosome conformation capture technologies have helped us gain significant insights into the folding principles of the mammalian genome, helping identify the short-range promoter-enhancer loops that underlie epigenetic regulation of gene expression, as well as long-range multi-loop domains that form sub-chromosomal chromatin compartments (Dekker et al., 2013; Denker and de Laat, 2016). While these technologies have helped define important rules related to the patterns of cis-folding of the cellular genome, their implementation in studying the trans-interaction between the genome of an invading DNA virus and its target cell remained largely unstudied. In this protocol, we describe the procedure for studying the interactome of the parvovirus Minute Virus of Mice (Figure 1). Owing to its 5 kilobase genome, the application of a modified Circular Chromosome Conformation Capture (4C) assay combined with high-throughput sequencing (Van De Werken et al., 2012) allowed the generation of a high resolution map of the sites on cellular DNA where MVM localizes during infection (Majumder et al., 2018).

### Procedure

  1. Parasynchronization and infection of cells
    1. Plate 5 x 106 cells at a concentration of 0.5 x 106 cells/ml in 10 ml Isoleucine-minus media in 15 cm dishes for 36-42 h at 37 Â°C and 5% CO2. Murine A9 fibroblasts and human NB324K kidney cells are permissive to MVMp infection and can be parasynchronized by isoleucine deprivation. 
    2. Release cells into G1/S-phase by aspirating the media and adding 10 ml complete DMEM media. Simultaneously, infect with MVMp virus at an MOI of 5 (25 Âµl of MVMp at 1 x 106 Pfu/Âµl) for 16 h.
      Note: MVMp replication starts as cells enter into S-phase, approximately 10 h post-release into complete DMEM.

  2. Crosslinking and lysis
    1. Crosslink cells in the dish by adding 540 Âµl of 37% formaldehyde (final concentration of 2%). Mix well by pipetting and incubate at room temperature for 10 min on a rocker (12 oscillations/min). 
    2. Quench the crosslinking reaction by adding 1.425 ml of 1 M glycine (final concentration of 0.125 M). Mix well by pipetting and incubate at room temperature for 5 min on a rocker (12 oscillations/min).
    3. Gently scrape cells using a disposable cell scraper (by moving horizontally, and vertically) and collect the cell suspension (in 12 ml media, formaldehyde and glycine) in 15 ml Falcon tubes. 
    4. Centrifuge at 500 x g for 5 min at 4 Â°C in a refrigerated centrifuge. Aspirate supernatant with a vacuum manifold or gently with a pipette.
    5. Wash cells with 10 ml PBS.
    6. Centrifuge at 500 x g for 5 min at 4 Â°C in a refrigerated centrifuge. Aspirate supernatant.
    7. Suspend pellet in 5 ml cold 3C Lysis buffer (freshly supplemented with protease inhibitors) and incubate on ice for 10 min.
    8. Centrifuge at 3,000 x g for 5 min at 4 Â°C. Aspirate the supernatant. A small white pellet should be visible at the bottom of the tube. This is mostly made up of cell nuclei. The tube can be snap frozen in liquid nitrogen at this point and stored at -80 Â°C. Thaw samples on ice if starting from this step.
      Note: Aspiration should be performed using a vacuum manifold. In its absence, pipettors may be used to carefully remove the supernatants without disturbing the cell (or nuclear) pellet.

  3. Primary restriction digest
    1. Suspend nuclei in 500 Âµl 1.2x NEB buffer 2.1 (60 Âµl of 10x stock diluted in 440 Âµl ddH2O) and transfer to Eppendorf tube.
    2. Add 7.5 Âµl of 20% SDS (final concentration of 0.3%) and place tube in a shaker at 37 Â°C at 900 rpm.
      Note: This process permeabilizes the nuclei. However, the formation of bubbles or too much SDS affects enzyme activity. Therefore, avoid forming bubbles, and ensure proper concentration of SDS solution.
    3. Incubate for 1 h at 37 Â°C while shaking at 900 rpm.
    4. Add 50 Âµl of 20% Triton X-100 (final concentration of 2%).
      Note: Prepare 20% Triton solution well in advance, mix and keep at 56 Â°C to ensure homogeneous solution. Otherwise, SDS is inadequately sequestered, leading to premature inactivation of the restriction enzyme.
    5. Incubate for 1 h at 37 Â°C while shaking at 900 rpm.
    6. Take out 10 Âµl aliquot as undigested gDNA Control. Store at -20 Â°C.
    7. Add 20 Âµl (400 U) of HindIII to remaining sample and incubate overnight at 37 Â°C while shaking at 900 rpm.
    8. Add 15 Âµl (300 U) of additional HindIII and incubate on a 37 Â°C shaker at 900 rpm for 4 h.
    9. Take out 10 Âµl aliquot as digested DNA Control. Store at -20 Â°C.
    10. Reverse crosslinks and determine digestion efficiency as follows:
      1. Add 2 Âµl of Proteinase K to the undigested DNA and digested DNA tubes, and incubate at 56 Â°C for 1 h. 
      2. Dilute the samples to 100 Âµl with 90 Âµl ddH2O. 
      3. Add 100 Âµl of phenol:chloroform:isoamyl alcohol (25:24:1) and vortex (setting: 10) for 10 s.
      4. Centrifuge at 13,000 x g at room temperature for 5 min. 
      5. Transfer supernatant to new Eppendorf tube and add 1 ml isopropanol, and 2 Âµl glycogen. Vortex thoroughly for 10 s.
      6. Centrifuge at 13,000 x g at room temperature for 5 min and aspirate the supernatant. A white pellet should be visible at the bottom of the tube.
      7. Add 1 ml of 70% ethanol to the tube.
      8. Centrifuge at 13,000 x g at room temperature for 5 min and aspirate the supernatant.
      9. Resuspend pellet in 20 Âµl Buffer EB. 
      10. Perform qPCR (using Bio-Rad SYBR Green Supermix; annealing temp: 60 Â°C) with 2 Âµl of undigested and digested gDNA using the primer sets P1-P2 (Input) and P1-P3 (undigested). Set up each qPCR reaction in triplicate.

  4. Ligation
    1. Add 40 Âµl of 20% SDS (final concentration of 1.6%) to the digested chromatin. 
    2. Incubate for 20-25 min at 65 Â°C.
    3. Transfer digested nuclei to a 50 ml Falcon tube.
    4. Add 6.125 ml of 1.15x Ligation buffer (10x concentrated buffer is diluted in ddH2O).
    5. Add 375 Âµl of 20% Triton X-100 (final concentration of 1%).
    6. Incubate for 1 h at 37 Â°C while shaking gently.
    7. Add 1 Âµl T4 DNA Ligase (100 U total) and incubate for 4 h at room temperature.
      Note: Can also perform the ligation reaction at 16 Â°C.
    8. Add 15 Âµl of 20 mg/ml Proteinase K (final 300 Î¼g).
    9. Incubate at 65 Â°C overnight to reverse crosslinks.
      Note: We also de-crosslink at 56 Â°C. Any temperature within this range reverses protein crosslinks without affecting the DNA.

  5. DNA purification
    1. Add 10 Âµl of 10 mg/ml RNase A (final 100 Âµg).
    2. Incubate for 30-45 min at 37 Â°C.
    3. Add 7 ml phenol-chloroform and mix by vortexing (setting: 10).
    4. Centrifuge for 5 min at 2,000 x g at 4 Â°C.
    5. Collect the upper aqueous phase, transfer to a new tube, add 7 ml chloroform, vortex (setting: 10).
    6. Centrifuge for 5 min at 2,000 x g at 4 Â°C.
    7. Transfer supernatant into a 50 ml tube, add 25 ml isopropanol and 5 Âµl glycogen (10 mg/ml). 
    8. Mix and place at -80 Â°C for 1 h.
    9. Centrifuge for 20 min at 4,200 x g at room temperature.
    10. Remove supernatant and add 10 ml 70% ethanol.
    11. Centrifuge for 15 min at 4,200 x g at 4 Â°C.
    12. Remove supernatant and dry pellet at room temperature.
    13. Dissolve DNA pellet in 200 Âµl of Buffer EB.

  6. Secondary restriction digest
    1. Set up NlaIII digest on all of the 3C DNA overnight at 37 Â°C with 25 Âµl of 10x Cutsmart buffer and 15 Âµl of NlaIII.
    2. Inactivate NlaIII by incubating at 65 Â°C for 20 min.
    3. Add 1x Phenol:Chloroform:Isoamyl alcohol (250 Âµl); vortex, spin (10,000 x g), collect supernatant.
    4. Add 250 Âµl chloroform, vortex, spin (10,000 x g), collect supernatant.
    5. Add 700 Âµl of isopropanol and 2 Âµl glycogen (10 mg/ml), vortex, spin (10,000 x g to precipitate), aspirate supernatant.
    6. Add 1 ml of 70% ethanol, spin (10,000 x g), aspirate supernatant.
    7. Resuspend in 1 ml of ultrapure water.

  7. Circularization
    1. Set up ligation reaction overnight with 1.5 ml of 10x ligase buffer, 12.5 ml of water, 1 ml resuspended NlaIII digested DNA, 1 Âµl T4 DNA ligase in a 50 ml Falcon tube.
    2. Add 15 ml Phenol:Chloroform:Isoamyl alcohol, vortex, spin (4,200 x g), collect supernatant.
    3. Add 15 ml Chloroform, vortex, spin (4,200 x g), collect supernatant.
    4. Add 25 ml Isopropanol and 5 Âµl glycogen (10 mg/ml) to precipitate DNA. Spin at 4,200 x g.
    5. Wash with 10 ml of 70% ethanol; spin at 4,200 x g and aspirate supernatant.
    6. Air dry the pellet at room temperature for 20 min. 
    7. Resuspend in 200 Âµl Buffer EB.
    8. Purify with QIAGEN PCR purification kit and elute twice in 100 Âµl Buffer EB.

  8. Inverse PCR
    1. Quantify V3C-seq product by NanoDrop and load 200-600 ng of template DNA per PCR.
    2. Set up inverse PCR using Platinum Taq DNA Polymerase High Fidelity from Thermo Scientific as follows:

      ddH2O

    4. Dilute PCR product 1:100 in TE buffer.
    5. Set up nested inverse PCR reactions with diluted PCR product as template.
    6. Purify nested inverse PCR product with PCR purification kit (QIAGEN) and elute in 50 Âµl Buffer EB.
    7. NanoDrop inverse PCR product and generate sequencing library with NEBNext Ultra II DNA Library Prep Kit for Illumina.
    8. Pool up to 12 samples per lane and sequence samples by high throughput sequencing using the Illumina NextSeq 500 platform.

### Data analysis

  1. Align the sequencing reads using Bowtie 2
    1. Module load Bowtie2/Bowtie2-2.2.9
    2. Bowtie 2 --trim5 20 --very-sensitive -x mm10 -S mvm_1.sam mvm_1.fastq
  2. Convert .sam files into .bam files and sort .bam files using SAMtools
    1. Module load SAMtools/SAMtools-1.3.1
    2. SAMtools view -b -S -o mvm_1.sam > aligned_mvm_1.bam
    3. SAMtools sort -o aligned_mvm_1.bam sorted_aligned_mvm_1.bam
  3. Compute histograms using BEDTools
    1. Module load BEDTools/BEDTools-2.26.0
    2. GenomeCoverageBed -ibam sorted_aligned_mvm_1.bam -bg -trackline -split -g ... > mvm_1.bedgraph
  4. Upload bedgraph file to UCSC genome browser
