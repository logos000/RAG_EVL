Self-organization Assay for Min Proteins of Escherichia coli in Micro-droplets Covered with Lipids

Authors: Shunshi Kohyama, Kei Fujiwara, Natsuhiko Yoshinaga, Nobuhide Doi

Abstract

The Min system determines the cell division plane of bacteria. As a cue of spatiotemporal regulation, the Min system uses wave propagation of MinD protein (Min wave). Therefore, the reconstitution of the Min wave in cell-sized closed space will lead to the creation of artificial cells capable of cell division. The Min waves emerge via coupling between the reactions among MinD, MinE, and ATP and the differences in diffusion rate on the cell membrane and in the cytoplasm. Because Min waves appear only under the balanced condition of the reaction-diffusion coupling, special attentions are needed towards several technical points for the reconstitution of Min waves in artificial cells. This protocol describes a technical method for stably generating Min waves in artificial cells.

Keywords: Spatiotemporal regulation, Artificial cells, Cell-sized space, Min system, Cell division, Reaction-diffusion coupling, In vitro reconstitution

Background

Min system, which determines the cell center for symmetric cell division, is one of the most striking examples of intracellular organization systems in bacteria (Rothfield et al., 2005; Rowlett and Margolin, 2013). Min system uses pattern formation of time-dependent protein gradient inside cells known as Min waves (Loose et al., 2008; Halatek and Frey, 2012; Bonny et al., 2013; Zieske et al., 2016; Kohyama et al., 2019). The Min wave is emerged by a reaction-diffusion coupling of two proteins: MinD and MinE. By binding to ATP, MinD forms dimer and attach to membrane. MinE is recruited to ATP-MinD and induces ATPase activity of MinD. By MinE, ATP-MinD becomes ADP-MinD, and are detached from the membrane. ADP-MinD is converted back to ATP-MinD by binding ATP in cytosol (Huang et al., 2003; Halatek and Frey, 2012). This sequence of reactions continuously proceed. Molecular diffusion of MinD and MinE are different between on membrane and in cytosol. By coupling the difference of molecular diffusion and the continuous reactions, Min waves including pole-to-pole oscillation of MinDE proteins in vivo appears (Huang et al., 2003; Halatek and Frey, 2012; Bonny et al., 2013; Wu et al., 2015).

To characterize the Min wave behavior in vitro, its reconstitution system on 2D planar membranes has been developed (Loose et al., 2008 and 2011; Zieske and Schwille, 2013; Vecchiarelli et al., 2013 and 2016; Denk et al., 2018). The 2D system has shown that MinD, MinE, and ATP are necessary and sufficient for the Min wave emergence. Although the 2D system has contributed to elucidation of the Min wave characteristics, our recent study showed that the condition of the Min wave emergence is drastically shifted when Min proteins (MinD and MinE) are confined in cell-sized spaces (Kohyama et al., 2019; Yoshida et al., 2019). Therefore, characterization of the Min wave behavior under cell-sized confinement conditions will be necessary for emulating real traits of the Min wave in living cells. In this report, we introduce the method to emerge the Min wave in a cell-sized space made by water-in-oil emulsion.

### Procedure

  1. Sample preparation
    1. Expression of His-sfGFP-MinD and MinE-mCherry-His
      1. Transformation to introduce the expression plasmids.
      2. Gently mix 100 Î¼l of chemical competent cells of E. coli BL21-CodonPlus(DE3)-RIPL (Note 2) with 1-10 ng of pET15b-sfGFP-MinD or pET29-minE-mCherry-His (Note 1).
      3. On ice 30 min.
      4. Heat shock at 42 Â°C for 1 min.
      5. Chilled on ice again.
      6. Add 400 Î¼l of SOB medium to the cells, and incubate at 37 Â°C for 30 min.
      7. Plating LB agar medium with 100 Î¼g/ml Ampicillin (for MinD) or with 25 Î¼g/ml Kanamycin (for MinE).
      8. Incubate the plate at 37 Â°C for 12-18 h.
      9. Pick up a single colony of the plate and inoculate in 4 ml LB medium with 100 Î¼g/ml Ampicillin (MinD) or with 25 Î¼g/ml Kanamycin (MinE).
      10. Pre-culture cells in LB medium at 37 Â°C for 12-18 h.
      11. Transfer 2 ml of the pre-culture to 200 ml LB medium with 100 Î¼g/ml Ampicillin (MinD) or with 25 Î¼g/ml Kanamycin (MinE).
      12. Add IPTG to 1 mM final at the time OD600 reaches 0.1-0.2 (MinD) or 0.6-0.7 (MinE).
      13. Further cultivate cells at 37 Â°C, 180 rpm for 3-4 h (MinD) or at 16 Â°C, 120 rpm for 12 h (MinE).
      14. Transfer the cultivated culture to 50 ml centrifuge tubes and collect the cells by centrifugation at 8,000 x g at 4 Â°C for 2 min.
      15. Store the cells at -80 Â°C after removal of supernatant if the experiment should be stopped at this point.
    2. Purification of His-sfGFP-MinD
      1. Suspend the stored cells expressing His-sfGFP-MinD in 4 ml LS-ADP buffer.
      2. Disrupt cells by sonication using Sonifier Analog Series at duty 30%, output 3, for 30 min (Note 3).
      3. Collect supernatant of the disrupted cells after centrifugation at 20,000 x g at 4 Â°C for 30 min.
      4. Mix the supernatant with 500 Î¼l of cOmplete His-Tag purification resin at 4 Â°C for 30 min under gently rotating.
      5. Load the resin onto a polyprep chromatography column.
      6. Wash the loaded resin with 25 ml WS buffer.
      7. Elute the target protein with 2 ml EL buffer.
      8. To exchange the EL buffer with storage buffer with 0.2 mM ADP, transfer the eluted solution to AmiconUltra-15 30k filters.
      9. Add 8 ml of storage buffer.
      10. Centrifuge the device at 4,000 x g at 4 Â°C. Concentrate the solution less than 2 ml (generally, 30-60 min continuous centrifugation is needed).
      11. Repeat Steps A2i-A2j (total 5-6 times).
      12. Transfer the concentrated solution to AmiconUltra-0.5 30k filters.
      13. Centrifuge the device at 14,000 x g at 4 Â°C to concentrate the solution less than 0.1 ml (generally, it takes 30 min).
      14. Estimate protein purity and concentrations by CBB staining after separating by SDS-PAGE (see quantification by CBB staining) and bicinchoninic acid (BCA) assay (according to the manufacturerâs procedure).
      15. Adjust protein concentration of the purified protein at 100 Î¼M by the storage buffer and store the protein at -80 Â°C before usage (Note 4).
    3. Purification of MinE-mCherry-His (Note 5)
      1. Suspend the stored cells expressing MinE-mCherry-His in 4 ml LS buffer.
      2. Disrupt cells by sonication using Sonifier Analog Series at duty 30% (cycles of 0.3 s ON and 0.7 s OFF), output 3 (out of 10), for 30 min (Note 3).
      3. Collect supernatant of the disrupted cells after centrifugation at 20,000 x g at 4 Â°C for 30 min.
      4. Mix the supernatant with 500 Î¼l of cOmplete His-Tag purification resin at 4 Â°C for 30 min under gently rotating.
      5. Load the resin onto a polyprep chromatography column.
      6. Wash the loaded resin with 25 ml WS buffer.
      7. Elute the target protein with 2 ml EL buffer.
      8. Dilute the elution fraction with 5- to 10-fold HG buffer.
      9. Apply the diluted solution to HiTrap Q HP column equilibrated with IEX-A buffer by using AKTA start.
      10. By using an ion exchange protocol (basically installed in AKTA start as âIEX exchangeâ), wash the column loaded with IEX-A buffer, and elute proteins by gradient mixing of IEX-A and IEX-B buffer.
      11. Determine the peak fractions of MinE-mCherry-His by SDS-PAGE, and use the peak fractions further.
      12. To exchange the buffer with Storage buffer, transfer the eluted solution to AmiconUltra-15 10k filters.
      13. Add 8 ml of storage buffer.
      14. Centrifuge the device at 4,000 x g at 4 Â°C. Concentrate the solution less than 2 ml (generally, 30 min-60 min continuous centrifugation is needed).
      15. Repeat Steps A3m-A3n (total 5-6 times).
      16. Transfer the concentrated solution to AmiconUltra-0.5 10k filters.
      17. Centrifuge the device at 14,000 x g at 4 Â°C to concentrate the solution less than 0.1 ml (generally, it takes 30 min).
      18. Estimate protein purity and concentrations by CBB staining after separating by SDS-PAGE (see quantification by CBB staining) and BCA assay (according to the manufacturerâs procedure).
      19. Adjust protein concentration of the purified protein at 100 Î¼M by the storage buffer and store the protein at -80 Â°C before usage (Note 4).
    4. Washing BSA
      1. Take 100~200 mg BSA in 15 ml centrifuge tubes.
      2. Add the 10 ml of MQ water to the tube and dissolve BSA by vortexing.
      3. Transfer the BSA solution to AmiconUltra-15 50k filters.
      4. Centrifuge the device at 4,000 x g at 4 Â°C to concentrate the solution less than 2 ml (generally, it takes 30-60 min).
      5. Add 8 ml of RE buffer.
      6. Repeat Steps A4d-A4e (total 5-6 times).
      7. Concentrate the solution less than 2 ml by centrifugation at 4,000 x g at 4 Â°C.
      8. Transfer concentrated solution to AmiconUltra-0.5 50k filters.
      9. Centrifuge the device at 14,000 x g at 4 Â°C for 30 min to further concentrate the solution less than 0.3 ml.
      10. Estimate protein concentrations by BCA assay (according to the manufacturerâs procedure).
      11. Store the BSA solution in the storage buffer at RT before usage (typically 300-400 mg/ml. BSA solution should renew once in a month.
    5. Protein quantification by CBB staining
      1. Load samples and dilution series of BSA (200, 400, 600, 800, 1,000 ng) on 12.5% SDS-PAGE gels (by 12.5% or 15% acrylamide with 1/37.5 of N,N'-Methylenebisacrylamide for His-sfGFP-MinD or MinE-mCherry-His, respectively).
      2. Run SDS-PAGE (typically, 20 mA, 60 min).
      3. Stain the gels by CBB solution at room temperature for 30 min.
      4. Remove redundant dye by immersing the gels in water at room temperature for 30 min
      5. Take the image of CBB stained gels by ChemDoc touch MP.
      6. Measure band intensities of BSA by Fiji software to construct a standard curve (Note 6).
      7. Quantify the amount of the sample loaded by measuring band intensity and using the standard curve (Note 6).
      8. Convert the amount to the concentration of proteins.

  2. Self-organization assay inside lipid droplets
    1. Preparation of lipid oil
      1. The general protocol for microdroplets preparation was followed according to a previous report (Fujiwara and Yanagisawa, 2014). Add 20 Âµl of 25 mg/ml E. coli polar lipid extract (dissolved in chloroform and stored at -30 Â°C, total 1 mg) into a round glass microtube (Maruemu Glass microtube No.2) (Figure 1A, Note 7).
      2. Dry up the lipid solution under gentle argon gas flow (Figures 1B-1D, Notes 7-9).
      3. Add 1 ml mineral oil to the dried lipid film to be 1 mg/ml (Note 7).
      4. Seal the glass tubes by DuraSeal.
      5. Sonicate the lipid tube to dissolve lipids with the mineral oil for 90 min at 60 Â°C by using Bransonic.
      6. Vortex the sonicated lipid oil for 1 min at room temperature.
      7. Store the lipid oil at room temperature and must use in the day. Do not chill on ice or storage in refrigerators.
    2. Microdroplets preparation
      1. Make the reaction mixture consisted of 1 ÂµM (0.58 mg/ml) His-sfGFP-MinD, 1 ÂµM (0.38 mg/ml) MinE-mCherry-His, 2.5 mM ATP, 100 mg/ml washed BSA in RE buffer in a 0.2 ml tube.
      2. Add 100 Î¼l of lipid oil into a 0.6 ml tube and pour 2 Î¼l of the reaction mixture to the lipid oil (Figure 2A).
      3. Gently tapping the tube about 10 times for emulsification (Figure 2B).

    3. Observation of self-organization of Min proteins inside the microdroplets
      1. Prepare two cover glasses (we use 25 mm x 36 mm and 18 mm x 18 mm) to make a coverslip chamber by using a double-sided tape (about 90 Âµm thickness) as spacers (Figure 3).
      2. Gently place a portion of the emulsion (15 Î¼l) into the coverslip chamber (Note 10).
      3. Observe self-organization of Min proteins inside the droplets by fluorescent microscope by 5-20 s intervals for 5 min or more at room temperature (ca. 25 Â°C). Generally, the exposure time was set to 500 ms for both GFP and mCherry. Generally, Min waves are very stable and continue at least 10 h. Thus, we can observe Min waves in many different (at least 10) positions with one coverslip chamber. A representative result is shown in Video 1.

### Data analysis

  1. Image analysis
    1. Examination of Min wave emergence and its patterns
      1. Open the tiff image file using Fiji software. Typically use 2,048 x 2,048 pixels, 16-bit, two-color channels for sfGFP-MinD and MinE-mCherry and time-lapse stack image.
      2. Select âImageâ âAdjustâ and click the âResetâ button for every two-color channels (âResetâ button automatically adjusts the contrast between the minimal pixel intensity and maximum pixel intensity included in the object image).
      3. If the contrast of the image is too low (usually high pixel intensity derived from protein aggregation cause this phenomena), manually adjust the contrast using âAdjustâ submenu.
      4. Run the time lapse movie and confirm whether Min protein waves appear or not [generally use at least 16 sequence images with 20 s intervals (total 5 min) to judge the wave appearance and wave patterns].
      5. Classify the waves into 5 patterns by the behavior of His-sfGFP-MinD (traveling wave, standing wave, inhomogeneous membrane localization, homogenous membrane localization and homogeneous cytosol localization) as describes below.
        1. Traveling wave: sfGFP-MinD continuously moves along the membrane of artificial cells. Typically traveling wave moves 1.5-2 rounds per 5 min under 1 ÂµM His-sfGFP-MinD and 1 ÂµM MinE-mCherry-His condition.
        2. Standing wave: sfGFP-MinD alternately appears at each side of cell pole (axis of poles are stochastically chosen). Typically, indicates 2-3 rounds per 5 min under 1 ÂµM sfGFP-MinD and 1 ÂµM MinE-mCherry condition.
        3. Inhomogeneous localization: sfGFP-MinD inhomogeneously localizes on membrane (typically occupies about half surface area of the inner membrane) but does not indicates dynamic movements within 5 min.
        4. Homogeneous membrane localization: sfGFP-MinD homogeneously localizes on membrane and does not move.
        5. Homogeneous cytosol localization: sfGFP-MinD homogeneously localizes inside droplets (cytosol) and does not move.

### Notes

  1. Sequence information of His-sfGFP-minD and MinE-mCherry-His are shown in Supplementary document. We are ready to share the plasmids if the reader have interest use those.
  2. RIPL strain may be replaced with other strains with (DE3) such as original BL21(DE3).

  4. Flash freezing is not needed.

  8. Because homogeneous dried film is preferable, slow drying (30 s or more to complete drying) by gentle gas flow is better.

  10. We recommend fast transfer of the mixture to the glass slide slip as soon as possible, although the time does not matter as we tested.
