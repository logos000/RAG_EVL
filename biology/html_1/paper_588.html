The protocol to remove high abundant proteins from HIV-1 infected human plasma

Authors: Sushanta Kumar Barik, Dr Keshar Kunja Mohanty, Deepa Bisht, Partha Sarathi Mohanty, Shripad Patil, Rananjaya Singh, Beenu Joshi, Srikanth Prasad Tripathy, Tej Pal Singh, Rekha Tandon, Sheetal Tomar, Srikanta Jena
Abstract:

Removal of high abundance protein from human plasma is an advantage during the analysis of protein in SDS-Poly acrylamide  gel electrophoresis. We have adopted a protocol for removal of high abundance protein from human plasma by following several previous protocols. The 50% TCA-Acetone is most suitable method for removal of high abundance proteins like albumin and globulin from HIV-1 infected human plasma samples. We have developed this protocol in our laboratory for carrying out the protein work from HIV-1 infected human plasma samples. This protocol is suitable to run the purified human plasma protein for sodium dodecyl sulfate polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis.  



Keywords: HIV-1, Human plasma, TCA, Acetone, SDSPAGE

Introduction

The human plasma is a rich source of proteins that leads to biomarker discovery for detection and diagnosis of a disease. The 289 proteins wereidentified in human plasma by two dimensional and mass spectrometry.1 The proteins must be up regulated or down regulated in human plasma during the seroconversion period in HIV infected patients or antiretroviral therapy users. These identification of differentially regulated proteins chosen as a suitable marker for detection of opportunistic infection in HIV infected patients has been already published.2 Most proteins were involved in HIV infection would be considered as new therapeutic targets against AIDS3..Several plasma protein purification by chemical methods like ammonium sulfate, isopropanol, chloroform:methanol, ,TCA-Acetone, chromatographic methods and several manufacturers kit were used for  depletion of high abundance proteins from human plasma samples. To achieve reliable cost, the TCA-Acetone is a better option to use for depletion of high abundance proteins from human plasma samples.

Procedure

Purification of human plasma proteins for SDS Poly-acrylamide gel electrophoresis: 

1. Initially 100 µl of plasma was taken in 1.5 ml eppendorf tubes in 5 replicates.

Then 1000 µl each of 10 to 50% TCA-Acetone was added to each of 100 µl plasma samples in eppendorf tubes.

2. The tube was kept at -200C for overnight.

3. Then the tube was centrifuged at 15000g for 10 minutes.

4. The supernatant was discarded and 90% acetone was added.

5. Then the tube was kept in -200C for 10 minutes.

6. The tube was centrifuged at 15000g for 10 minutes.

7. The supernatant was discarded and kept it for drying.

8. Finally 50 to 500µl rehydration buffer was added in to the dried pellet.

9. After human plasma protein purification, the protein was resolved in a 10% running gel and 5% stalking gel and stained with 0.2% coomassiae brilliant blue R250 solution. Then the gel was clearly visualized with a proper de-staining solution. The gel image was taken by geldoc (Biorad, USA). 

Troubleshooting

After centrifugation, the pellet should not be disturbed. Albumins and globulins were removed from the tubes through gentle pipetting. This is the crucial step in purification. 

Time Taken

The time taken for the procedure would be 18 hours. 

Anticipated Results

SDS-PAGE analysis indicated that 50% TCA-Acetone protocol is suitable for human plasma protein purifications as major unwanted proteins are removed.Ethanol was first used for depletion of high abundance protein from plasma samples before few decades 4.Then after few years, ammonium sulfate was used for depletion of albumin and globulin from plasma samples5. Chromatographic methods were adopted for depletion of abundance proteins from plasma samples6.Several manufacturers were developed removal of 7 to 20 high abundance proteins from plasma samples based on the type of disease detection for diagnosis.To reliable the cost effective, TCA-acetone is the best choice for depletion of high abundance proteins from HIV-1 infected plasma samples and is suitable HIV-1 infected for plasma samples analysis in SDS-polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis. This protocol is suitable for clinical applications for electrophoresis of human plasma proteins.
