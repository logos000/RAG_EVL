Summary

Here, we describe a detailed protocol for the isolation of purified populations of viable spermatogenic cells derived from the non-human primate model organism Macaca fascicularis (cynomolgus). Using fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS), we describe methods to isolate spermatogonia and primary spermatocytes ranging across the sub-stages of meiosis prophase I. These cell populations can be used with a variety of downstream assays, including single-cell approaches such as RNA sequencing, chromatin immunoprecipitation, quantitative RT-PCR, and immunocytochemistry.

For complete details on the use and execution of this protocol, please refer to Lau et al. (2020).

Highlights

  • Dissection of macaque testis and dissociation of tissue to single cells
  • Isolation of primary spermatocytes with FACS using Hoechst 33342 fluorescence
  • Sample preparation and gating strategy for obtaining SSEA-4-positive spermatogonia

Before you begin

Preparation of BD FACS Aria III Cell Sorter for analysis and sorting

  1. Flow cytometric analysis and fluorescence-activated cell sorting were performed on a BD FACS Aria III Cell Sorter equipped with a 488 nm blue, 561 nm yellow green, 640 nm red, and 355 nm UV laser.
  2. Warm the lasers on the BD FACS Aria III Cell Sorter for a minimum of 20 min before calibrating the system with BD FACS Diva Cytometer Setup & Tracking (CS & T) beads. This ensures that time delay calibration was properly setup prior to all flow cytometry experiments.
Note: Time delay calibration must be done prior to all experiments to ensure that laser delay values are updated for accurate generation of flow cytometry results.
  1. Quality control for cell sorting experiments was performed using the BD FACS Accudrop beads to ensure that at least 95% beads were deflected to the left stream before the initiation of all cell sorting experiments.
  2. Unless otherwise stated, PI was excited using the 561 nm yellow green laser and its emission captured using a 610/20 bandpass (BP) and 600 long pass (LP) filter. Emission from APC off the 640 nm red laser was detected using a 670/14BP filter. Hoechst blue and Hoechst red fluorescence emitted from excitation of Hoechst 33342 was detected off the 450/50BP coupled with a 410LP filter and 670 LP/630 LP filter respectively.
  3. Cells were sorted at 70 psi with a 70μm nozzle. Sort streams were aligned and sorted into tubes using the 4-way purity precision mode.
Note: Sort precision modes on the sorter may differ between different brands of instruments. If in doubt, select the precision mode that allows you to obtain the highest purity that your experimental needs require without compromising yield.

Step-by-step method details

Testis dissection

Timing: 30 min

The following steps are carried out to isolate seminiferous tubules from the macaque testis for enzymatic digestion.

  1. Perform testis dissection is on a 10 cm petri dish (Figure 1).
    1. Remove the outer layers, e.g., skin and loose connective tissue.
    2. Detach the epididymis.
  2. Weigh the testis.
  3. Remove the tunica albuginea.
    1. Make a small incision of the tunica albuginea.
    2. Dissect under it to create a dissection plane, separating the seminiferous tubules underneath from the tunica.
  4. Cut the testis tubules into 1 - 2 g pieces, and further cut each piece into smaller pieces to facilitate subsequent digestion.

Dissociation of testis samples

Timing: 1 h

The subsequent steps are performed to obtain a single-cell suspension of unselected cells from seminiferous tubules using enzymatic digestion.

  1. Submerge testis tubule pieces in 25 mL of Tissue Digest Buffer A and leave the samples to digest at 37°C in a water bath for 5 min.
    1. Shake the tube vigorously every 2 min until loosened tubules become visible.
  2. Remove and discard the supernatant.
Optional: Cells in the supernatant, i.e., primarily interstitial cells, may also be kept and processed separately if this is of interest.
  1. Add 25 mL of Tissue Digest Buffer B and allow the digest to occur for 30 min at 37°C in a water bath.
    1. Shake the tube vigorously every 2 min until dissociation of the tubules into a single-cell suspension of cells.
  2. Add 2.5 mL of FBS to stop digestion.
  3. Centrifuge the cell suspension at 200 × g for 5 min at 4°C and discard the supernatant.
  4. Resuspend the cell pellet in 25 mL of PBS
  5. Repeat steps 9 and 10.
  6. Centrifuge the cell suspension at 200 × g for 5 min at 4°C and discard the supernatant.
  7. Resuspend the cells in a desired volume of PBS.
  8. Filter the cell suspension through a 40 μm cell strainer into a fresh tube.
  9. Count the cells to determine cell concentration.

Sample preparation for spermatocyte fluorescence-activated cell sorting

Timing: 45 min

Steps 16–20 describe live cells staining with the Hoechst 33342.

  1. Aliquot the appropriate number of cells into four tubes.
    1. Unstained control: 1 × 106 cells
    2. PI only control: 1 × 106 cells
    3. Hoechst 33342 only control: 1 × 106 cells
    4. PI + Hoechst 33342 sample: 5 × 106 to 1 × 107 cells
Note: Tubes a - c serve as single stained controls used for gating and assessing fluorescence spectral overlap purposes. Assessment of the spectrum of spermatogenic cells can then be gated and analyzed using Tube d.
  1. Centrifuge the cell suspension at 200 × g for 10 min at 4°C and discard the supernatant.
  2. Resuspend the matching cell pellet tube with 1 mL of prepared stain solution.
  3. Incubate tubes at 37°C for 30 min in the dark. Agitate tubes every 10 min to resuspend cells thus ensuring uniform staining.
Note: Incubation with Hoechst 33342 stain has to be performed for at least 30 min at 37°C for optimal resolution.
  1. Tubes are kept on ice, protected from light and filtered using a 40μm cell strainer prior to analysis.

Sample preparation for spermatogonia fluorescence-activated cell sorting

Timing: 45 min

Steps 21–28 describe live cell staining with SSEA-4 APC-conjugated antibody for the isolation of spermatogonia.

  1. Aliquot the appropriate number of cells into three tubes.
    1. Unstained control: 1 × 106 cells
    2. PI only control: 1 × 106 cells
    3. PI + SSEA-4 APC-conjugated Ab sample: 1 × 107 cells
Note: Tubes a and b serve as single stained controls used for gating and assessing fluorescence spectral overlap purposes. Tube c contains all fluorochromes of interest involved in the cell sorting assay.
  1. Centrifuge the cell suspension at 200 × g for 10 min at 4°C and discard the supernatant.
  2. Resuspend the matching cell pellet tube with 1 mL of prepared SSEA-4 APC-conjugated Ab stain solution. Incubate tubes at 37°C for 30 min in the dark. Agitate tubes every 10 min to resuspend cells.
  3. Centrifuge the cell suspension at 200 × g for 10 min at 4°C and discard the supernatant.
  4. Resuspend cell pellet and wash twice with DPBS to remove unbound SSEA-4-APC-conjugated antibodies.
  5. Resuspend cells with DPBS + 10% FBS at a density of 2 to 3 million cells/mL for the sort.
  6. Add 1 μg/mL of PI and filter the stained cell suspension using a 40μm cell strainer prior to cell sorting.
  7. Tubes are kept on ice and protected from light before cell sorting.

Gating strategy for isolating primary spermatocytes

Timing: 20 min

Steps 29–34 describe the gating strategy to use on the BD FACS Diva software for isolation of primary spermatocytes at various meiotic sub-stages of prophase I.

  1. Create an FSC-A versus SSC-A contour plot (linear scale). Draw a “Scatter” gate to include cells of interest while excluding debris.
  2. On the FSC-A versus FSC-H contour plot (linear scale), create a sub-gate under the “Scatter” gate to encompass single cells. This step helps to omit doublets which could otherwise contaminate purity of the sorted cells.
  3. Next, gate on PI negative live cells under the Singlets Gate using a PI-A (log scale) versus FSC-A (linear scale) contour.
  4. Display all live viable events on a DNA histogram with Hoechst blue on the x axis and count on the y axis. Low Hoechst blue intensity events are excluded to omit debris and unstained cells. Draw a “DNA” gate to only include cells containing DNA content of 1C and greater. Data are collected in linear scale.
  5. View gated “DNA” events on the Hoechst red-A/Hoechst blue-A contour plot (linear scale). A spectrum of cells in varying stages of spermatogenesis can be identified and gated based on their DNA content and Hoechst red/Hoechst blue fluorescence profile (please refer to Figure 2F for more details on the profile obtained).
  6. Create 4 gates and label them A, B, C, and D. These gates represent cell populations that are enriched in primary spermatocytes at pre-leptotene, leptotene/zygotene, early pachytene, late pachytene meiotic stages respectively (Figure 2A).
Note: PI is a nucleic acid stain that enters dead cells with disrupted cell membranes, and is excluded from live ones with intact membranes. The live cell dye Hoechst 33342 on the other hand is able to enter cells through diffusion and binds to the nucleic acid residues on the minor groove of DNA. Hoechst allows the detection variation in DNA content and chromatin, and in meiotic cells Hoechst red/blue fluorescence can be used to discriminate subpopulation of cells undergoing meiosis I (Bastos et al., 2005). Similarly, in this protocol, PI is used to reliably discriminate live cells (i.e., intact PI negative cells) versus non-viable (PI positive) cells, and Hoechst fluorescence is used to discriminate on the DNA content of meiotic cells.
Note: Similar Hoechst 33342 staining profiles can be obtained using different brands of flow cytometers equipped with the laser lines described above. Filter configurations can differ between different systems.

Flow cytometry analysis and fluorescence-activated cell sorting for primary spermatocytes

Timing: 40 min

In this section, testis cells are sorted according to their Hoechst blue/Hoechst red fluorescence. Gated primary spermatocytes at various meiotic prophase I sub-stages are collected from the sort.

  1. Using the gating template created above, acquire samples and optimize photomultiplier tube (PMT) voltages using the single stain controls in the following sequence (unstained, PI only, Hoechst 33342 only).
    Note: This step ensures that signals captured are on scale and also allows us to access fluorochrome interactions with one another (spectral overlap).
    1. Unstained tube
      1. Sets baseline voltages for the sample.
      2. Determines the level of autofluorescence in the sample without adding any fluorochromes (Figure 2B).
      3. Select and gate for singlets (Figure 2C). Omit doublets which may contribute to false positive signals.
    2. PI only control tube
      1. Allows for the assessment of PI negative live and PI positive dead cells. Cell viability can be determined from the percentages of PI negative cells that did not stain with propidium iodide (Figure 2D).
    3. Hoechst 33342 only control tube
      1. Determines the spread of cells based on their nucleic acid content on the Hoechst blue histogram (Figure 2E).
      2. Bi-parameter display of Hoechst blue/Hoechst red fluorescence signals allows for the assessment of cells in varying stages of spermatogenesis.
  1. Acquire and record 10,000 to 50,000 events in these tubes.
  2. Check the Hoechst profile of viable (PI negative) cells using the Hoechst 33342 + PI-stained tube using the optimized PMT settings above.
    1. Record a minimum of 500,000 events before establishing gates for primary spermatocytes.
    2. Define and gate cells based on their Hoechst fluorescence pattern, which corresponds to their DNA content (Figure 2F). The shift in Hoechst red and Hoechst blue corresponds to the meiotic progression of primary spermatocytes, which allows for the discrimination of sub-stages of meiotic prophase I.
      1. Gate 4N primary spermatocytes, based on their distinct sub-stages, following the contours of the cell density.
  1. Program to collect and sort cells.
    1. Sort cells using a low flow rate with 70 μm nozzle at 70 psi. Maintain event rate around 2,000–3,500 events/s.
      Note: High flow rates can increase the speed of cell sorting but compromise sort purity of the isolated fractions.
    2. Maintain a minimum of >80% sort efficiency during the sort. Monitor discard rate throughout to ensure that is it preferably not >200 events/s.
  1. Collect sorted cells in round bottom FACS tubes pre-loaded with collection media.
Note: It is important to pre-coat the interior of collection tubes with collection media as this reduces the chances of sorted cells sticking to the interior of the tube during collection. 10% FBS in collection media enhances the survival of sorted fractions.
  1. To store cells, centrifuge to pellet collected cells at 200 × g for 10 min at 4°C.
  2. Discard supernatant and resuspend cells in Freezing Media to a concentration of 1000 cells/μL.
  3. Aliquot 100–200 μL of cell suspension in cryovials and store at −80°C.

Gating strategy for isolating spermatogonia

Timing: 20 min

Steps 43–46 describe the gating strategy to use on the BD FACS Diva software for isolation of SSEA-4 positive spermatogonia.

  1. Create an FSC-A versus SSC-A contour plot (linear scale). Draw a “Scatter” gate to include cells of interest while excluding debris.

  3. Next, gate on PI negative live cells under the Singlets Gate using a PI-A (log scale) versus FSC-A (linear scale) contour.
  4. Create 2 gates and label them SSEA4- and SSEA4+. These gates represent cells that are negatively or positively stained with anti-SSEA-4 APC-conjugated antibody (Figure 3A).

Expected outcomes

Fluorescence-activated cell sorting for primary spermatocytes

Hoechst blue fluorescence reflects variations in chromatin content/DNA content. Increasing Hoechst blue fluorescence intensity allows visualization of testicular cells in various stages of spermatogenesis based on their DNA content. Haploid spermatids containing 1N DNA content are located at the lowest part of the Hoechst blue scale nearest 50. Hoechst blue/Hoechst red contour plot allows distinction of cellular populations A, B, C, and D. Back gating of these individual clusters reveals that A corresponds to regions enriched in 2N to 4N early meiotic prophase I cells such as pre-leptotene, and B corresponds to 4N leptotene and zygotene spermatocytes. Region C and D corresponds to early and late pachytene spermatocytes containing 4N DNA content respectively (Figure 2F).

Quality control of sorted spermatocytes using immunofluorescence

Spermatocytes isolated may be further analyzed and classified into sub-stages of prophase I of meiosis, based on their immunostaining patterns for γ-H2AFX (green) and SYCP3 staining patterns (Figure 4A). Cells undergoing leptonema exhibit SYCP3 aggregates and abundant intense γ-H2AFX staining (Gaysinskaya and Bortvin, 2015). During zygonema, the formation of long SYCP3 stretches are observed in the cell, with decreased levels of γ-H2AFX, as it becomes restricted to chromatin of the remaining axial elements to synapse and start to associate with the condensing X and Y chromatin (Gaysinskaya and Bortvin, 2015; Mahadevaiah et al., 2001). For pachytene cells, γ-H2AFX is only present on the condensed X and Y chromatin, as referred to as the sex body. This transcriptionally silenced sex chromatin is typically observed at the nuclear periphery (Chi et al., 2011; Mahadevaiah et al., 2001, 2008). Finally, during diplonema, γ-H2AFX remains on the sex body as it observed toward the middle of the nucleus (Mahadevaiah et al., 2001).

Limitations

Hoechst 33342 staining is extremely sensitive to deviations from the intended protocol. Slight variations in the amount of Hoechst 33342 dye added and incubation timings of the dye with dissociated testicular cells may influence the overall Hoechst profile derived. We also recommend that a minimum incubation timing of 30 min at 37°C with the dye to obtain the Hoechst blue/red profile described above. Additionally, previous studies have shown that the Hoechst incubation timing and profile can differ between different species of animals (Bastos et al., 2005; Gaysinskaya and Bortvin, 2015). Hence, the protocol for Hoechst staining presented should be used as a reference in species other than an adult cynomolgus macaque.
